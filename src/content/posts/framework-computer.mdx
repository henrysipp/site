For fifteen years now I have used Macintosh computers near-exclusively. 



 Snow Leopard [startup sequence](https://youtube.com/watch?v=5DSXz9xst5k) 

Over the course of the next decade and a half I became religiously comfortable with the Apple computing environment and ecosystem. I didn't care that it was a walled garden, this was the computing utopia we were promised and everything "just worked".  

But that's just it, I became comfortable with the ecosystem and blinded to clear issues of [growing pains](https://appleinsider.com/articles/22/07/19/apple-agrees-to-50m-settlement-in-macbook-butterfly-keyboard-lawsuit) in a post-Jobs world. Granted with the creation of Apple Silicon it feels that the company has come leaps and bounds since the late 2010's. While they have regained their status as hardware kings however, it has become increasingly clear that the business direction has changed dramatically as implied by their blatant [contempt for developers](https://daringfireball.net/linked/2025/06/05/apple-appeal-denied-antisteering) and major [losses of trust](https://stratechery.com/2025/apple-and-its-intelligence-gaps/) in software commitments and platform stability.
